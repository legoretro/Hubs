RBC

Start with size, color, shape, inclusions, and the reticulocyte response.

First Pass

  • Size: microcytic, normocytic, or macrocytic.
  • Color: central pallor and hemoglobinization.
  • Shape: poikilocytosis points toward membrane, fragmentation, or marrow stress.
  • Response: reticulocytes help separate production problems from loss/destruction.

Simple BOC Mindset

  • Match CBC indices to smear findings.
  • Do not overcall one rare cell; look for a pattern.
  • Ask if the marrow is responding appropriately.
  • Use morphology as a clue, not the whole diagnosis.

WBC

Keep the differential clean: lineage, maturity, quantity, and reactive clues.

First Pass

  • Neutrophils usually point toward bacterial inflammation or stress patterns.
  • Lymphocytes help with viral/reactive patterns and chronic lymphoid processes.
  • Eosinophils often connect to allergy, parasites, or drug reactions.
  • Blasts or very immature cells move the question toward marrow disease.

Smear Language

  • Report what is seen: left shift, toxic change, atypical lymphocytes, blasts.
  • Correlate with WBC count and differential percentage.
  • Use morphology to decide what needs confirmation.
